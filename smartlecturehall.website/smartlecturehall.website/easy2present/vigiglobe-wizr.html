Analyzing social media content in real time

Founded in 2011, Vigiglobe has its headquarters on the French Riviera with additional offices in Belgium and the United Kingdom. Financed in part by US$2 million in Series A investment capital, the company has quickly become a leader in real-time social media analytics. Vigiglobe CEO Leendert de Voogd says the founders saw an opportunity to analyze not only what was being said on social media, but also the context in which it was being discussed.

 

Analyzing the context is especially important because people can use the same words to convey different meanings and messages. The Vigiglobe team created proprietary algorithms to accurately interpret and contextualize social media messages in real time.

 

Using Vigiglobe WIZR™ and On Demand software-as-a-service offerings, customers can monitor and measure their online reputations, assess new product launches or marketing campaigns, and analyze how people compare their brands to those of competitors. They can then use this real-time information to make well-informed business decisions.

 

Incorporating advanced visual analysis 

Vigiglobe was doing an effective job of interpreting the text in social media messages and providing customers with rich information about the content and context of those messages. The technology recognizes all spoken languages and provides full analytics for seven of the most widely used, with more languages coming soon. But de Voogd says the company soon realized that people were sharing more and more visual images on social media. “Between 30 and 50 percent of all social media messages today contain pictures,” he says. “We had to find a way to accurately interpret those pictures to provide our clients with insightful data.”

 

The company examined several options for image analysis, but they were either too limited or unable to provide real-time information—until Vigiglobe heard about the Computer Vision API from Microsoft Cognitive Services. “Our criteria were efficiency, reliability, and richness of content, and Microsoft Cognitive Services met these requirements,” de Voogd says.

 

Vigiglobe is using the Computer Vision API to differentiate itself from other companies that claim to offer image recognition and interpretation. Says de Voogd, “We want to contextualize images on social media: what images are being shared, what are people communicating or commenting on? We would not be able to provide this rich analysis without the Computer Vision API.”

 

As an example, de Voogd says the Computer Vision API from Microsoft gives Vigiglobe the ability to accurately infer the age and gender of people from their social media images and messages.

“We can give our clients a more accurate picture of the people who are tweeting or posting messages about them,” he says.  

 

Analyzing images for a deeper understanding

With Microsoft Cognitive Services, Vigiglobe and its customers are benefitting from more accurate data, better content control, richer insights, and improved interoperability.

 

  • Accurate and reliable data

de Voogd praises the accuracy and reliability of the data that the Microsoft Computer Vision API provides. “I have not seen anything comparable,” he says. “We can use the Computer Vision API to prove to our clients the reliability of the data, so they can be confident making important business decisions based on that information.”

 

  • Filtered and moderated content

When Vigiglobe launches a marketing campaign for a customer, the company often asks people on Twitter or Facebook to use a specific hashtag to join the conversation. Because it’s public, Internet trolls can misuse the hashtags to share their own content, which may not be suitable for children.

 

But with Microsoft Cognitive Services, Vigiglobe has more control over the content. “The Computer Vision API works to identify and filter out problematic adult content,” de Voogd says. “That gives us another valuable service we can offer our clients.”

 

  • Cross-platform interoperability

The APIs from Microsoft Cognitive Services are cross-platform by design. “Our solutions have to work in every environment and on every platform,” says de Voogd, who notes that the Vigiglobe technology supports iOS, Android, Windows, and Linux. “The work Microsoft has done with these APIs is remarkable. We didn’t experience any major problems using the Microsoft Computer Vision API with our solutions—it was very easy.”

 

Vigiglobe is looking forward to future innovations from Microsoft Cognitive Services. “We chose to work with Microsoft because we were looking for a partner that keeps us moving in the right direction,” de Voogd says. “Finding a community of experts that can help us grow is critical to our success.”
